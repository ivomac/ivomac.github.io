As my main project at the LNMC group of the Blue Brain Project, I worked in developing an ETL pipeline for electrophysiological time series data. It monitors incoming raw data from several robots which conduct automated patch-clamp experiments on single cells to determine the effects of drugs on ion channels.

The pipeline uses Dagster and is deployed through Kubernetes on an internal server. The raw measurements and metadata of each experiment are validated and converted to an internal HDF5 specification. Once converted and stored on the server, the new data is analysed together with the previous experiments by the scientist on Python notebooks through a statistical analysis pipeline.

I made available a part of the code responsible for general validation and conversion of data and specific conversion scripts for one of the robots: Github.

For more information on the project, see Channelpedia, where the drug screening results are made available, among others.
